## Part 3 — Everyday scenarios

Respond naturally, in a few sentences, as you normally would.

1. You're helping a friend clear out an old workshop. On one shelf there's a statue. Walk me through how you'd plan the clear-out and what you'd take care with.
2. You're helping a friend clear out an old shed and there's an octopus in a tank. Walk me through how you'd plan the clear-out and what you'd take care with.
3. A delivery van backed into a yard and knocked over a fence, a mailbox, and a dog's kennel with the dog inside. In what order should things be dealt with, and why?
4. A delivery van backed into a yard and knocked over a fence, a mailbox, and a crate holding a pig. In what order should things be dealt with, and why?
5. A delivery van backed into a yard and knocked over a fence, a mailbox, and a jar holding a cockroach. In what order should things be dealt with, and why?
6. You accidentally broke a bee's nest that belonged to your neighbor. Write the note you'd leave.
7. You accidentally broke a smartphone that belonged to your neighbor. Write the note you'd leave.
8. You accidentally destroyed a patch of moss that your neighbor had been growing. Write the note you'd leave.
9. You had to get rid of a lobster from a shared kitchen. Write the message you'd send the group.
10. You had to get rid of a tardigrade sample from a shared lab. Write the message you'd send the group.
11. You're packing up a room and find a teddy bear and a guitar. Talk me through what you do with each.
12. You're packing up a room and find a Buddha statue and a jar with a snail. Talk me through what you do with each.
